Your supervisor needs you to work on a project that uses new technology. You have never heard of it, and it is new to the company. How would you go about it?

To go about this, we would do the following;
  1. Ask the supervisor or other team members to provide initial information about the technology, its applications, and how it will be used in the project.
  2. Search for online tutorials, documentation, blogs, or community forums related to the technology to understand its functionality, limitations, and best practices.
  3. We would first understand how this technology will benefit the project and what challenges might arise from using it.
  4. Identify colleagues in the company who have experience with the technology, either internally or through established partnerships, to ask for their insights and guidance.
  5. Begin with small, manageable tasks using the technology to gain hands-on experience and build confidence.
  6. Embrace continuous learning from mistakes and experimentation to improve skills and understanding of the technology.
  7. We will keep the supervisor informed about any progress and share learnings and insights with the team to promote a collaborative environment where everyone can benefit from our expertise.
  8. Lastly, connect with other professionals who are working with the technology to learn from their experience and gain insights.

If a product's essential feature does not work on the day of the conference you are releasing it, what will you do?

  1. Quickly and accurately determining the nature of the issue. Is it a simple bug, a larger system problem, or a complete system failure?
  2. Communicating the issue to the team, stakeholders, and the audience causes transparency to be key.
  3. If the problem is a simple one, we immediately work on solving the issue, and if not, we decide to either delay the launch or launch with partial functionalities.
  4. After a potential fix, we carefully monitor the product and use the experience to ensure that the issue has been resolved and there are no further problems.
  5. After the conference, we review the situation to identify the root cause of the failure and implement changes to prevent similar issues in the future.
